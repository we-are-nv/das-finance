Insurance and Protection at DAS Finance Southend

It’s important to realise that everyone faces risks in their future and that safeguarding the financial security of your family and dependents is of paramount importance.

With this in mind, you may want to make provisions to protect your families from financial liabilities in the unfortunate event of death, critical illness or unemployment. We have extensive experience in reviewing and establishing protection arrangements to cover an array of eventualities.

‍

Our advisers, researchers and administrators regularly review and implement all types of insurance and protection.

Due to the fact that we are completely independent, we are able to review protection products across the whole of the marketplace. We can therefore provide an impartial service which is bespoke and uniquely tailored towards your individual requirements and circumstances.

‍

Mortgage Protection Insurance (MPI) is a type of insurance policy that helps your family to continue to meet your monthly mortgage payments if you – the policyholder and mortgage borrower – die before your mortgage is fully paid off. Some MPI policies will also offer coverage for a limited time if you lose your job or become disabled or injured after an accident.

Some companies call it mortgage life insurance because most policies only pay out when the policyholder dies.

‍

Mortgage Protection Insurance isn’t a mortgage requirement. Whichever type of loan you choose at DAS Finance, you can buy a home without paying for MPI. Though your lender may recommend a policy, it’s completely up to you whether you decide to buy.

FAQs

Why should I get Insurance for my Mortgage?

Often, lenders will still be able to recover their investment by repossessing your home, in the event of your death or serious illness. Surprisingly, most lenders do not insist on you taking out insurance to protect against these risks. For your own peace of mind, and that of your dependants, we highly recommend insuring your life and your income which should include critical illness cover.

What Insurance do I have to take out?

Your lender will insist on you taking out buildings insurance cover and having their interest in your property noted in the policy.  If anything should happen to destroy or damage your property, it will obviously lose value and your lender would not be able to recover their investment, if it is uninsured.

How do you calculate mortgage affordability?

The days of lenders just lending triple your salary, without consideration to individual circumstances, are thankfully gone. Mortgages are written on a basis of your affordability, which will theoretically reduce the number of people having their homes repossessed. In simple terms, your incomings and outgoings are considered to give a figure which will represent the amount of ‘ available’ money you have.

What’s an agreement in principle?

By calculating your affordability we can assess the current mortgage market and find the best deal for you. A formal mortgage offer will not be given until a valuation of the property to be purchased is carried out, but an ‘agreement in principle’ allows you to go ‘property shopping’ knowing how much you can borrow.

What if I haven’t found a house yet?

This doesn’t present an issue; we can give you an ‘ agreement in principle’ which will allow you to look at properties within your budget and make an offer with the security that you have funding in place. There is nothing more disappointing than finding your dream home and discovering you can’t get the funding to purchase it. We will find you a mortgage based on affordability.

Are you regulated by the FCA?

We are regulated and directly authorised by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA)

Can my high street bank provide me with a mortgage?

In today’s competitive markets, we are all used to shopping around for everything from our weekly groceries to our electrical appliances. Why wouldn’t we apply the same process to financial products? Chances are your bank won’t offer you the best deal that is available in the mortgage market, and when you consider the length of time you are repaying a mortgage, small savings per month add up to significant figures over the term of the mortgage. If your bank is offering the best deal in the market, you won’t miss out, because we will recommend it as a result of our extensive market search.

Why shouldn’t I try to find my mortgage myself?

You can, but it will not only take up a lot of your time, it could, depending on where you are   researching, have a negative effect on your credit history with multiple credit searches being recorded on online mortgage portals.

WHEN DOES MY LENDER NEED MORTGAGE LOAN INSURANCE?

Lenders will require mortgage loan insurance if a borrower has a down payment of less than 20% of the purchase price of the home.

WHO PAYS FOR THE MORTGAGE LOAN INSURANCE?

Like any other kind of insurance, there are premiums to be paid. The lender typically passes on the cost of insurance to the borrower. The premiums can be paid up front in a lump sum or blended in with your mortgage loan payments.
